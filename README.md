# Digital Choice Manifesto: Engineering the Behavioral Digital Twin

*A structural framework for algorithmic behavior, privacy, and digital choice architecture.*

## 1. The Executive Vision

In the era of algorithmically mediated choices, static consumer models fail to capture the dynamic, intertemporal nature of human decision-making. The **Digital Choice Manifesto** proposes a paradigm shift: leveraging **Behavioral Digital Twins** to map structural econometrics onto digital footprint data. 

By grounding AI-driven agentic simulations in rigorous microeconomic foundations, we can predict how variations in digital choice architectures, privacy constraints, and algorithmic nudges impact consumer welfare.

## 2. Core Pillars

### I. The "Marginal Complier" in Digital Markets
Drawing from structural discrete choice experiments (where up to 45% of populations exhibit "neutral trust" swing behavior), we introduce the concept of the **Trust Buffer** into digital economics. How do digital platforms mathematically optimize for these marginal consumers, and what happens when algorithmic targeting exploits temporal vulnerabilities?

### II. Privacy Asymmetry & The Degradation of the Digital Twin
When privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) or platform-level changes (e.g., Apple's ATT) restrict digital trace data, algorithms lose precision. We model how this degradation of the Digital Twin disproportionately impacts the "swing" demographic, altering market equilibrium and platform revenue generation.

### III. Intertemporal Algorithmic Nudges
Digital environments explicitly engineer choices that alter users' discount rates ($\delta$). This framework incorporates structural time preferences to predict long-term digital fatigue, addiction, and engagement under varying algorithm designs.

## 3. Architecture & Methodology

This repository serves as the staging ground for deploying offline, quantized LLMs (leveraging breakthrough Leech Lattice Vector Quantization) to simulate digital markets with millions of independent, structurally-parameterized AI agents.
