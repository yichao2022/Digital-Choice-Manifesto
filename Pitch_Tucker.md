# Targeted Outreach Strategy: Prof. Catherine Tucker (MIT Sloan)

**Target Profile:** 
Catherine Tucker is the Sloan Distinguished Professor of Management and a Professor of Marketing at MIT Sloan. Her research intersects digital economics, privacy, algorithm bias, and digital platforms.

**Strategic Hook:**
Connect your "Behavioral Digital Twin" and "Intertemporal Choice" research to her focus on *digital privacy* and *algorithmic targeting*. We position your structural model as a way to mathematically evaluate the consumer welfare impacts of the digital platforms she studies.

## Draft: Cold Email / Pitch

**Subject:** Postdoc Inquiry - Behavioral Digital Twins, Privacy Asymmetry, and Digital Choice Architecture

Dear Prof. Tucker,

I have long followed your work on digital economics, particularly how privacy controls and algorithmic bias reshape digital platforms and consumer welfare. I am writing to express my strong interest in joining your research group as a Postdoctoral Researcher, bringing a novel structural framework to model these exact dynamics.

I am a PhD candidate at UT Dallas defending this April. My Job Market Paper develops a structural model of intertemporal choice, quantifying how behavioral barriers (what I term the "Trust Buffer") disproportionately affect "marginal compliers"—the highly sensitive swing groups often ignored by static models.

I am currently expanding this framework into the **Digital Choice Manifesto**, engineering "Behavioral Digital Twins." By grounding quantized LLM agents in rigorous structural econometrics, this framework can simulate how changes in digital choice architecture (e.g., shifting privacy regulations like ATT, or intertemporal algorithmic nudges) alter market equilibrium and exploit or protect these marginal consumers. 

Given your unparalleled leadership in the economics of digital data and privacy, I believe my methodology—bridging structural econometrics with agentic behavioral simulation—could offer a powerful new lens for your ongoing research on platform design and algorithm regulation.

I have attached my CV and Job Market Paper for your review. My public repository and architectural drafts for the Digital Twin framework can be found on my GitHub. I would be thrilled to have a brief conversation about how my computational and economic toolkit could contribute to your lab's agenda.

Thank you for your time and pioneering work in this field.

Best regards,
